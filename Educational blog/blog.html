What is Artificial Intelligence?

Published on November 30, 2025 | Category: Fundamentals | Reading time: ~8 min

Artificial intelligence (AI) is the intelligence of machines or software, as opposed to the intelligence of humans or animals. It is a field of study in computer science that develops and studies intelligent machines.

Definition

The term "artificial intelligence" was coined in 1956 by John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Nathaniel Rochester, and Claude Shannon at the Dartmouth Conference. AI research deals with the question of how to create computers that are capable of intelligent behaviour.

In practical terms, AI applications can be divided into:

  • Narrow AI (or weak AI): Systems that are capable of performing specific tasks (e.g., facial recognition, internet search, self-driving cars).
  • General AI (or strong AI): Hypothetical systems that possess the ability to understand, learn, and apply intelligence across a wide variety of tasks at a level equal to or beyond a human being.
  • Artificial Superintelligence (ASI): Hypothetical AI that surpasses human intelligence in virtually every field.

Key Subfields

  • Machine Learning (ML)
  • Deep Learning
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP)
  • Computer Vision
  • Robotics
  • Expert Systems

Goals of AI Research

Researchers aim to develop systems that can:

  1. Reason, use strategy, solve puzzles, and make judgments under uncertainty
  2. Represent knowledge and make deductions
  3. Plan and learn from experience
  4. Understand and generate natural language
  5. Perceive and interact with the physical world

Ethical Considerations

Modern AI development raises important ethical questions such as bias in algorithms, job displacement, privacy, autonomous weapons, and the long-term risk of superintelligent AI.
